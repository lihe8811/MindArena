# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students and academics: debate club members, university students, and people preparing for competitions or exams requiring argumentation. They come to MindArena to practice arguing a position, sharpen their logic, identify their rhetorical weaknesses, and see measurable improvement over time. They use the app in focused sessions, often alone, with clear goals around Elo, logic scores, or technique.

## Product Purpose

MindArena is an AI-powered rhetoric and debate training platform. Users practice structured debates against AI opponents across rigor levels, receive real-time analysis (fact checking, logic auditing, fallacy detection), and track skill growth through Elo ratings and performance radar charts. Success looks like a student returning weekly, noticing their logic score climbing, and feeling genuinely better-equipped to argue in class or competition.

## Brand Personality

Curious, exploratory, open. The platform invites intellectual play rather than projecting aggression or elite gatekeeping. There is a Duolingo-like sense of progress and delight — small wins acknowledged, growth made visible — but without cartoon mascots or juvenile gamification loops. The minimalism is alive, not sterile.

## Anti-references

- **Cartoon-heavy gamification**: Extreme Duolingo aesthetics with mascots, animation overkill, or dopamine-trap streaks. The gamification is real but understated.
- **Corporate SaaS cold minimalism**: Notion/Linear gray-on-white sterility. Too emotionless for a learning product.
- **Esports / gaming dark mode**: Neon RGB, aggressive angles, terminal-hacker aesthetics. Too intimidating; pushes away curious newcomers.
- **Academic institution**: Library beige, formal serif, university portal energy. Too stiff to feel like a personal practice tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Curious, not combative** — The interface invites exploration rather than intimidates. Debate is a thinking practice, not a battlefield. Language, iconography, and layout should signal openness.
2. **Earn every level** — Progression and achievement feel earned, not manufactured. Logic scores, Elo changes, and stage completions are surfaced with clarity and weight. No hollow confetti.
3. **Minimal but alive** — Clean layout with purposeful moments of delight and personality. Whitespace is generous; color is used sparingly but warmly. Not sterile.
4. **Show the thinking** — The analytical engine (fallacy detection, logic scores, AI feedback) is the product's core value. Surface it clearly and make it feel illuminating, not overwhelming.
5. **Progressive depth** — Casual users see a welcoming practice space; expert users discover depth (rigor controls, philosophical frameworks, Elo breakdown). Never front-load complexity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text at 4.5:1 minimum contrast, large text at 3:1. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations. The product has no known color-vision-specific requirements, but avoid encoding critical state in color alone.
